# Data Governance and Access to PII

## Introduction
Data governance refers to the framework of policies, processes, and standards that ensure data is managed effectively, securely, and responsibly within an organization.

## Importance of Data Governance
Data governance is essential for:
- Maintaining data quality and consistency
- Ensuring compliance with legal and regulatory requirements
- Supporting accurate decision-making
- Protecting sensitive information

## Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
PII refers to any data that can be used to identify an individual. Examples include:
- Names
- National ID numbers
- Email addresses
- Phone numbers
- Financial information

## Access Control for PII
Access to PII must be strictly controlled to prevent misuse and data breaches. Key practices include:
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Data encryption
- Authentication and authorization mechanisms
- Regular audits and monitoring

## Risks of Poor Governance
Without proper governance:
- Data breaches may occur
- Organizations may face legal penalties
- Trust with customers may be lost

## Conclusion
Effective data governance ensures that PII is handled securely and responsibly, reducing risks while enabling organizations to derive value from their data.
